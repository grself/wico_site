"TAPTICO" LAST OF THE WICOMICO.... The book is now ready...the cost is $48 which
incudes the cost of mailing. The book is well formatted for easy locating your ancestors. The book traces the Native American Taptico/Tapp lineage from 1678 to the 5th and 6th and 7th generation. For those that buy the book will receive all future research on the Native American Tapps free of charge. I currently have many of the Tapps identified up to the 1930s,50s however The book is appr 380 pages.
For those that buy the book and you are a descendant of King William Taptico, you will receive a certificate that states that you are a member of the Wicomico Tribe.
Previously the system that we had was cumbersome, we received an application then we attempted to determine if the person was a descendant, with the book a person should be able to determine immeadiately if they are descendants. In the event that you are an adopted Tapp this is not a problem. Two things that the Bureau of Indian Affairs has made clear, DNA is not required and if you were adopted by a Tapp family that is of Indian lineage you will be accepted, however let me make it clear, the person applying for membership in the tribe must prove that he/she was adopted.If the adoption took place in the 1880s and 1700s, adoption papers are not available in most cases, if this is the case, a complete lineage must be forwarded.

WINGAPO CHESKCHAMAY ! (WELCOME ALL FRIENDS)

Chamah Netab ( Welcome Friend)
we are in the process of selecting council members in order to bring the tribe back to life. The Council will be structured more to the times and designed to make decisions quickly. Our primary and number one project will be to seek State Approval in Virginia, much of this work has previousley been completed......... we previously submitted the first book of the Wicocomico History to indian Affairs, the second book will be submitted with a copy of the first book, to the State of Virginia. Some of our previous projects included build a Culture center, issuance of a stamp honoring King William Taptico, (this was done) and a time capsule. We did have approval to erect a monument to the Wicocomico Indians, however the selection appeared to be to restrictive and only available to a select few. At the time of disbanding the council, we were in the process of having a historical marker placed on the road that at one time, part of the Wicocomico land.
We welcome any and all to apply for membership in the Wicocomico Nation.
